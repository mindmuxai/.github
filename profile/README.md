# MindMux

> **Your projects remember.**  
> The open project brain for AI-native builders and teams.

---

## What we believe

AI turned every builder into a small team. But every new chat, agent, and tool still starts from zero context. Product decisions, architecture trade-offs, rejected options, and hard-won lessons stay trapped in scattered docs, issue threads, and individual memory.

**Context is the scarce resource of the AI era.**  
The builders and teams that manage it will outlearn and outexecute everyone else.

We don't think the answer is another chat wrapper or another document app. The answer is a *project brain*: a structured, portable, human-and-agent-readable memory layer that evolves with your work.

---

## MindMux — The open project brain

MindMux is a **local-first AI workbench** that turns your project conversations into a structured brain you actually own.

**Chat → Brain → Task**

- **Chat** with Claude, GPT, Gemini, or your own agent inside a native desktop workspace.
- **Brain** distills decisions, architecture, and context into plain Markdown in a `brain/` folder.
- **Task** hands off executable work to Claude Code, Codex, GitHub Issues, and more — with the full context attached.

Your brain lives as Markdown files on your local machine: portable, diffable, and yours forever.

---

## Our path to open

We believe AI-native teams deserve an open, portable standard for project knowledge — not another walled garden.

We are progressively open-sourcing two things:

1. **The Project Brain Standard** — an open specification for how project knowledge is structured, linked, and consumed by humans and agents.
2. **The MindMux client source code** — so anyone can audit, run, and extend the workbench that brings the standard to life.

Open standards build trust. Trust is the only real moat in the age of AI.

---

## Why now

The unit of work has changed:

> **People × Agents × Tools × Teams**

Every project now needs a memory layer that humans and agents can read, update, and trust together. Existing tools were built for the old unit — documents, tasks, code. We are building for the new one: **context**.
